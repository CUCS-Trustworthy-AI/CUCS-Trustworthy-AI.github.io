Five pillars of trustworthy AI

Our faculty's research spans the foundations and systems that make modern AI dependable in the settings where it matters most.

01

Safe

Models that behave reliably under distribution shift, adversarial pressure, and real-world deployment.

02

Secure

Defenses against prompt injection, data poisoning, and supply-chain attacks on AI systems.

03

Measurable

Benchmarks, audits, and evaluation methods that turn abstract claims about AI into quantifiable evidence.

04

Purpose-driven

AI systems designed to serve specific human and scientific goals — not capability for its own sake.

05

Interpretable

Models whose decisions can be inspected, explained, and challenged by the people they affect.
